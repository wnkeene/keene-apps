EPIC

Browse NASA's best images, videos, and live streams from one universal app.

Platform: iOS 26.0+ • iPadOS 26.0+ • macOS 26.0+ • visionOS 26.0+

What It Does

EPIC brings NASA's public imagery and video services together in one place. You can check today's Astronomy Picture of the Day, browse Earth imagery from NASA's EPIC camera, explore Mars rover photography, search NASA's image and video archive, watch live NASA TV when broadcasts are active, and save favorites for later.

Daily NASA Browsing

  • Today — View the Astronomy Picture of the Day, jump to other dates, or discover random picks from NASA's archive.
  • Explore — Browse curated sections for Mars, Earth, galaxies, nebulae, the ISS, the Solar System, and more.
  • Detail View — Open any item for a larger view, metadata, sharing, and download options.

Search, Save, and Download

  • Search NASA's Media Archive — Search NASA's public image and video library for missions, astronauts, planets, launches, and more.
  • Favorites — Save images and videos to your personal library for quick access later.
  • Downloads — Save standard or higher resolution media when the source asset is available.

NASA TV and Video

  • Live NASA TV — Watch NASA TV streams directly in the app when a live broadcast is active.
  • On Demand — Browse NASA videos by topic such as Artemis, James Webb, ISS spacewalks, launches, and press events.
  • Universal Experience — EPIC is designed for iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Vision Pro.

Content is not loading

EPIC depends on live NASA services for most content. If you are offline, NASA is rate limiting requests, or NASA's servers are temporarily unavailable, some sections may not load until you try again later. The app can fall back to a cached APOD entry when one is available.

NASA TV says "No Broadcast"

This usually means NASA is not currently running a live broadcast on that channel. Try again later or check NASA's published schedule for upcoming live events and launches.

Restore purchases

If you already bought Lifetime Unlock, open EPIC and use Restore Purchases from the paywall. Restores are handled by Apple's App Store and require the same Apple Account used for the original purchase.

Downloads and library data

On Mac, EPIC opens a save panel that defaults to your Downloads folder. On iPhone and iPad, the system file exporter lets you choose where to save the file. Favorites, download history, and saved APOD data are stored with Apple's SwiftData framework and may sync privately through your iCloud account when CloudKit is available.

Privacy

EPIC does not use analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking SDKs. The app stores library data with Apple's system frameworks and may sync that data through your private iCloud account when available. NASA content requests go directly to NASA's public services.
